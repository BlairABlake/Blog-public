Three Key Traits of Blockchain Explained

By BlairABlake 21 April 2023

Cryptocurrencies are now expanding their popularity ever than before. The price of dogecoin has skyrocketed due to Elon Musk. Web3 has been gaining massive traction from technological enthusiasts. Under the umbrella of the term DeFi, these applications geared to disrupt the current financial systems.

Behind the secne is the Blockchain that are working to make those technologies possible. So is it Blockchain that forms the future of the Internet? We can’t be certain. But we can be sure that Blockchain has the potential to form the future of the Internet. That’s primary due to the three traits of Blockchain, scarcity, censorship resistance, and data portability, which I’m gonna explain the overview and detail in the following sections.

Scarcity

The most popular application of blockahin is certainly cryptocurrency. You may know Bitcoin. That’s the first and foremost important application of blockhain. At the time of writing 1BTC worth about 30000USD. Backed by nothing why cryptocurrency has so much value (though not every cryptocurrency has so much value)?

Think in this way. If everyone can easily forege the money without legal sanction, does that money possess a value? No, definitely. Things need scarcity to be used as a money. That’s the reason why Bitcoin has such enormous value.

Now we have thougt why scarcity is needed at first. Let us now look at how Bitcoin achieved its scarcity.

The reason behind its scarcity is twofold. One is its distributed nature. Unlike centralized systems, currently dominating the world, blockchain is a decentralized service. Tens of thousands of nodes across the world are running the same blockchain and reaching the same states under the same ruleset. If you were to change one of the transactions, you have to convince tens of thousands of nodes to agree to that change, which is nearly impossible.

Second is its append-only nature. Once transacation is added to the blockchain, the next block will be chained to that block. Continuing this process if you were to alter transactions in some block, you need to alter every block toward that block, which is practically infeasible.

Censorship resistance

Simply said, cehsorhip resistance means that no single entity has the controll over the network. Everyone in the network has to follow the same rule.

This can be the cheif property for some people because no one including the government and organization cannot alter the blockchain. No one is the exception. This free us from confication.

Data portability

The idea of data portability is that there should be practically no friction of user’s porting data from one service to another.

Think of how much control do we have over our own data. We don’t have control in most of cases. It is large companies who possess our own data. But large companies in the techonology and finance space have not been great stewards of data. They have been breached and hacked. See this list of data being breached. Worse than this is that they have often attempted to conceal tehse breaches.

Leveraging the Blockchain we can own our own data.
